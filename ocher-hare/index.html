What this workshop is

A small, week-long creative program where kids learn simple design fundamentals and create their own sticker sheet and packaging.

They’ll draw, experiment, make decisions, and turn their ideas into something tangible they designed themselves — building creative confidence through the design process along the way.

What kids will do

Over one week, students will:

  • ● Learn simple design basics (color, layout, visual ideas)
  • ● Sketch and develop their own sticker concepts
  • ● Turn drawings into digital designs (optional iPad + Procreate)
  • ● Design and create packaging for their sticker sheet
  • ● Print, assemble, and present their work
  • ● End the week with a fun sticker trade day

What they gain

Creative confidence

Expressing their own ideas clearly through design

Follow-through & ownership

Completing a project from concept to finished product

Real design thinking skills

Learning color, layout, and how ideas turn into tangible work

A safe space to experiment

Trying, revising, and growing without fear of "getting it wrong"

About Me

Hi, I'm Seira — a designer, maker, and educator at heart.

I’ve worked in UX, product, and industrial design, and my journey began in early childhood education. I hold an associate degree in ECE and spent five years working directly with children, creating environments that nurture curiosity, independence, and creativity.

This workshop brings those experiences together in a small, supportive setting where kids can explore ideas confidently and experience the full creative process — from imagination to a finished piece they’re proud to take home.

Every child sees the world differently. My role is to guide that process and help them create something uniquely their own.

Program snapshot

Ages: 10 – 12 years old
Group Size: Small, guided setting
Format: Week-long summer workshop
Devices: iPad + pencil optional
Location: Berkeley / Albany / El Cerrito area (final TBD)
What they'll bring home: Sticker sheet or stickers with packaging

Safety & environment

This will be a structured, supportive, small-group environment focused on:

  • ● encouragement over perfection
  • ● exploration over pressure
  • ● process over "getting it right"

*Kids will be guided step-by-step and supported in a respectful, inclusive space where creativity feels safe.
